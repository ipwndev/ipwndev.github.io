Electri-city is a website regarding the design, construction, and experimenting with electrical circuits. These electrical circuits are designed to be easy to build, and hopefully have a practical use. In addition it serves to make the lives of engineers, physics students, and hobbyists lives easier by providing a singular resource regarding the construction of electronics circuits.


This site was designed, primarily by hand in the text editor nano on a Darwin based unix derivative. Outside libraries or "Dynamic Drives" are sourced below. This site is designed to run on the W3's HTML 5 Specification. it lacks compatibility with Internet Explorer 9 and under. It has been tested and works on Googles Chrome 41+, FireFox 3+, Apples Safari on 10.6.8+, and Internet Explorer 10+. The reason for not supporting older versions of internet explorer is due to the security risk. Microsoft has stopped supporting older versions of internet explorer in favor of newer browsers. The old versions are still open to numerous exploits and hacks. Even if you are running an older computer, Firefox and Chrome both work on older windows XP machines.
