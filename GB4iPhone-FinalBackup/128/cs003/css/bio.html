Boomer Band: Biography

Peter Butler - Guitar & Vocals

Born into a musical family, Peter Troy Butler started singing at the age of 3, playing drums at 11 and picked up guitar at 13. He played and sang in various rock bands, school bands and choirs through high school. During the early 80s, he worked with a Sacramento area club band named Slyder where he got a real taste of the “working band” experience. It was around that time that he got the recording bug and became engineer and co-owner of TFY recording in Citrus Heights. After a few other musical ventures, he was hired by a variety band named Spring Water Express, playing rock, jazz, pop and even some country covers. He worked with Springwater for 14 years, performing everything from weddings to small festivals to playing over 25 shows at the California State Fair. After a few side projects and stints with bands Out of the Blue and Voxville 5, Pete had the good fortune to be invited to play for a new band being put together by long time friend Chris Teresi. This rockin’ little group came to be known as Boomer. Pete says, “Besides the high level of musicianship, the thing I love most about playing in Boomer is the relaxed, low-key attitudes of the members with no egos to get in the way of the fun.” If you've been to a Boomer show, I'm sure you know what Pete's talking about!

Karla Davis - Vocals & Keyboards

Raised in a musical family, Karla started singing as a toddler and began performing duets at the age of five with her older sister. Throughout her youth, she was active in church choirs performing and directing, and the Rio Americano High School Madrigals, A cappella Choir and the school's musical theater arts program. Through the years she's been a member of the duo David and Karla, New Rules, The Kids, Mecca, Spring Water Express, and local show band Zazz, touring in the northwestern and eastern US and Canada. A former San Juan USD music educator and currently choir director and classroom teacher at Orangevale Open School, she enjoys the enrichment music education and performing brings, and it remains an integral part of her life. With an experienced voice and vocal range, she covers a variety of artists with versatility and ease.

Matt Brown – Drums

Matt’s musical journey began the first time he saw Ringo Starr playing drums with the Beatles on TV. He was 9 years old at the time and totally fascinated by the drums. He has been playing drums and percussion ever since! During his high school years he was exposed to all kinds of music through the music program at Elk Grove High School. At school he played with the Marching, Concert, and Jazz bands, as well as the Winter Percussion Ensemble where he was "center snare". His drumset training has come in the form of private lessons from teachers such as Tomm Roland (Professor of Percussion, University of Nebraska, Omaha) and Tony Dey (Sacramento area professional). Matt loved music and the world of percussion so much that he decided to major in it at college! In 2010, he earned a Bachelor's of Music degree, with a focus on Percussion Performance from CSU Sacramento. While studying at CSUS, Matt had the privilege and honor to study with Dr. Daniel Kennedy. It was Dr. Kennedy who exposed him to all areas of percussion, such as: timpani, marimba, vibraphone, xylophone, concert snare drum technique, Gamelan ensemble, and ethnic hand drums such as: tabla, riq, pandiero, djembe, and doumbec. It was also during his college years when he met his wife, Sarah (a fabulous clarinetist). They currently reside in a newly purchased home in Citrus Heights, and are celebrating the recent arrival of their first child!

Jeroen Smit - Guitar & Vocals

From the very beginning Jeroen was surrounded by music. His father was a conservatory violinist, singer and assistant conductor in a world-touring choir. So when Jeroen started playing guitar at age 7 and began to show some promise, he and his dad would play music together, mostly Hot Club style stuff and some then-contemporary run-of-the-mill. When he was 15, his family moved from their native Netherlands to Los Angeles, where Jeroen had his first go at playing jazz in the high school jazz band. As all these things were happening, Jeroen also played and sang in a number of rock/top-40 bands. One of the projects ended up getting him pretty close to a record deal and hanging out with a bunch of Hollywood types, but as most of these things go it ended up with a fizzle. These days Jeroen is the contemporary music director at his church, is a soloist for weddings, funerals and forced retirement of public official parties. And, of course, there’s Boomer. “This is probably the most fun I’ve had playing in a band,” Jeroen comments. “It’s nice being able to swap the vocal leads with not just one, but two other singers (Pete & Karla), plus always having the opportunity to lay down some fat harmonies. And to have the rock-solid backline of Chris and Matt makes the band groove like nothing else.” Come to one of the shows and see and hear what he’s talking about.

Chris Teresi - Bass

Born to a band director and a piano teacher, music has always been in Chris’s life. He started playing trombone in 4th grade and bass in 7th. He has played in bands and choirs at Folsom High school, and toured in Europe twice with the American River College jazz choir. Chris has also played in rock and blues bands from high school to the present. During the 80’s he played with a group of friends in Roadrunner and the Rick Adams Blues Band in the 90’s. A high point was playing in the band The Surfaris at the Santa Cruz Fairgrounds. Boomer’s three lead singers, great guitar players and a kickin’ drummer have made this the most fun band ever. He is also a co-owner of Northridge Music Center in Citrus Heights.
